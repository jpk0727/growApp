GROW: Automated and Research Oriented Aeroponic Agriculture

We are building on automated growing environment for optimizing crop production. Here you can monitor the conditions of our crops, analyze the data that is constantly being collected, and design and execute experiments on the actual system. This project is funded by Swarthmore College Department of Engineering created by Connie Bowen and Jess Karol for their Senior Engineering Design Project.

What it Means

Our growing environment monitors what the plants are currently experiencing. In order to optimize production, it is necessary that these parameters stay within a healthy ranges. Thus, we have feedback systems based on the sensors outputs to regulate these conditions, so you shouldn't see them vary too much. Regulation occurs by turning on the fans when the temperature or humidity gets too high, and kicking on the lights when there is not enough external light
